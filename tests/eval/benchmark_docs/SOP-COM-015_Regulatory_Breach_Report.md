## 1. Purpose

This procedure defines the reporting obligations, escalation timelines, and remediation requirements for regulatory breaches across all AVL operating jurisdictions.

## 2. Breach Classification

### Table 1: Regulatory Breach Severity Levels

| Level | Definition | Reporting Window | Mandatory Notification To |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|--------------------------|
| Critical | Potential criminal liability, >$1M in fines, or imminent safety risk | 2 hours from discovery | Regional VP, General Counsel, relevant regulator |
| Major | Civil penalty exposure $100K–$1M, or service suspension risk | 24 hours | Regional Operations Director, General Counsel |
| Minor | Administrative notice, <$100K exposure, no service disruption | 72 hours | Regional Operations Director |

## 3. Reporting Procedure

### 3.1 Initial Notification

Any AVL employee who becomes aware of a potential regulatory breach must report it immediately through one of these channels:
- AVL Compliance Hotline: 1-800-AVL-COMP (24/7, multilingual)
- AVL HR Portal: Form COM-015-BREACH (submitted to Global Compliance Office)
- Direct notification to the Regional Compliance Officer (contact list in Appendix A)

## 4. Document Retention and Legal Privilege

### 4.1 Privilege Handling

All breach-related documents prepared by or at the direction of legal counsel are subject to attorney-client privilege. The Global Compliance Office must flag these documents upon receipt and route them through the General Counsel's office within the notification window.

### 4.2 Retention Schedule

Breach documentation must be retained for a minimum of 7 years from the date of resolution, or longer if required by applicable jurisdiction, whichever is longer.
