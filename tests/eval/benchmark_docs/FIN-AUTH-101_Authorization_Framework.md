## 1. Purpose

This Finance Authorization Framework defines the spending authorities, budget variance thresholds, procurement approvals, and financial sign-off requirements for all divisions of AeroVelo Logistics Global (AVL).

## 2. Core Spending Authority Matrix

### Table 1: Per-Transaction Spending Limits

| Authorization Level | Max per Transaction | Max per Month (Cumulative) | Budget Variance Allowed | Approval Required Above Limit |
|--------------------|--------------------|---------------------------|----------------------|------------------------------|
| Field Logistics Coordinator | $500 | $2,000 | 0% | Supervisor |
| Hub Operations Manager | $5,000 | $20,000 | 5% | Regional Director |
| Regional Operations Director | $25,000 | $100,000 | 10% | Regional VP |
| Regional Operations Vice President | $100,000 | $500,000 | 15% | CFO (if >$75,000) |
| Chief Financial Officer | $500,000 | $2,000,000 | 20% | Board Risk Committee (if >$250,000) |
| Chief Executive Officer | Unlimited | Unlimited | 25% | Board of Directors |

### Table 2: Emergency Procurement Exceptions

Emergency procurement (defined as non-planned procurement required within 72 hours to maintain safety, regulatory compliance, or critical service continuity) is subject to the following:

1. **Up to $5,000:** Hub Operations Manager may authorize with post-facto finance review within 5 business days.
2. **$5,001 – $50,000:** Regional Operations Director must approve prior to commitment (verbal approval acceptable, written confirmation within 24 hours).
3. **$50,001 – $250,000:** Regional Operations Vice President must approve. Post-facto board notification required if cumulative emergency spending for the quarter exceeds the region's annual emergency contingency allocation (currently set at 3% of regional annual budget).
4. **Above $250,000:** Requires CFO pre-approval unless a Tier-4 incident as defined by SOP OP-204 is active, in which case the COO may authorize with simultaneous notification to the CFO and General Counsel.

## 3. Budget Variance Policy

### 3.1 Variance Tolerance

Each regional division receives an annual operating budget. The following variance tolerances apply:

- **Americas Division (Sarah Jenkins):** ±12% variance before CFO review required
- **EMEA Division (Marcus Vance):** ±10% variance before CFO review required  
- **APAC Division (Kenji Sato):** ±8% variance before CFO review required

### 3.2 Cross-Budget Transfers

Transfers between budget categories (e.g., from "equipment maintenance" to "emergency freight") exceeding 20% of the source category budget require Regional VP approval and notification to Global Finance.

## 4. Vendor Contracting Authority

### Table 3: Contract Value Thresholds

| Contract Annual Value | Required Approval | Contract Length Maximum | Competitive Bidding Required |
|----------------------|-------------------|------------------------|------------------------------|
| <$100,000/year | Regional Operations Director | 2 years | No (sole source permitted) |
| $100,000 – $500,000/year | Regional Operations Vice President | 3 years | 3 quotes minimum |
| $500,000 – $2,000,000/year | CFO + Regional VP | 5 years | 5 quotes minimum + RFP process |
| >$2,000,000/year | Board Risk Committee | Per board resolution | Full RFP process, independent evaluation panel |

### 4.1 Existing Contract Renewals

Contracts within 15% of previous contract value may be renewed at the same authorization level without competitive bidding. Contracts with >15% increase require full competitive re-procurement.

## 5. Incident-Related Financial Resolution

Financial resolutions arising from logistics incidents (fines, penalties, customer compensation, corrective action costs) follow a dual-track authorization:

1. **Incident-specific costs** are authorized per the authority levels defined in Section 3.1 of SOP OP-204 (Incident Management).
2. **Budget allocation** for those costs is pulled from the regional "Incident Reserve Fund," which is pre-allocated at 2% of each regional annual budget.
3. If the Incident Reserve Fund is depleted, the regional Operations VP must submit a variance request to the CFO before authorizing additional incident-related expenditures.
