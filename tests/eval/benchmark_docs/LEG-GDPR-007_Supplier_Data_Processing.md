## 1. Purpose

This policy defines AeroVelo Logistics Global's (AVL) obligations under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the UK Data Protection Act 2018 when engaging suppliers, vendors, or contractors that may process personal data of AVL employees, customers, or third parties on AVL's behalf.

## 2. Scope

Applies to all AVL procurement engagements involving suppliers operating in or transferring data into the EEA, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland. Cross-referenced by procurement document **AVL-PUR-012** (Supplier Qualification) and financial framework **FIN-AUTH-101** (Authorization Framework).

## 3. Supplier Classification for Data Processing

### 3.1 Data Processor Designation

Any supplier that stores, transmits, analyzes, or accesses personal data on AVL's behalf is designated a "Data Processor" under Article 28 GDPR. A signed Data Processing Agreement (DPA) is mandatory before any commercial transaction is initiated, regardless of contract value.

### 3.2 Sub-Processor Approval

Suppliers may not engage downstream sub-processors without AVL's prior written authorization. This requirement applies even to emergency procurement under **FIN-AUTH-101 Section 3** (Emergency Procurement Authority).

### 3.3 Country-Specific Provisions

| Region | Applicable Regime | Required Contractual Clauses |
|--------|-------------------|------------------------------|
| EEA (incl. Germany, Netherlands, France) | GDPR | EU Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) — 2021 set |
| United Kingdom | UK GDPR + DPA 2018 | UK International Data Transfer Addendum (IDTA) |
| Switzerland | revFADP | Swiss FDPIC-approved SCC adaptation |
| Other (Americas, APAC) | Local laws | AVL Standard Data Protection Addendum |

## 4. Emergency Procurement Override

In the event of an emergency procurement under FIN-AUTH-101 Section 3 ($5,001 – $50,000 bracket) involving an EMEA supplier:

> **A provisional Data Processing Agreement (DPA-PROV) must be executed within 72 hours of the verbal approval. Failure to file the DPA-PROV within 72 hours automatically voids the procurement authorization and triggers a Tier-2 compliance incident under SOP-COM-015.**

The provisional DPA template is maintained by the Office of the General Counsel and is available in the AVL Legal Document Repository (folder: `legal/templates/DPA-PROV/`).

## 5. EuroVolt GmbH — Pre-Approved Data Processing Status

**EuroVolt GmbH (Supplier ID: EUR-001)** has a fully executed Master Data Processing Agreement (MDPA) on file, executed 2024-06-12. Emergency procurement from EuroVolt GmbH does **not** require a provisional DPA, provided:

1. The procurement scope falls within the existing MDPA service categories (power distribution units, technical schematics, ancillary maintenance records).
2. No new personal data categories are introduced.
3. The Regional Compliance Officer (Dr. Klaus Richter for EMEA) is notified within 24 hours.

Procurement outside these conditions reverts to the standard 72-hour provisional DPA requirement.

## 6. Breach Notification Coordination

A personal-data breach involving an AVL supplier must be reported under **two** parallel reporting tracks:

1. **Regulatory:** the relevant Data Protection Authority within 72 hours (GDPR Article 33), coordinated by the General Counsel.
2. **Internal:** the AVL Compliance Hotline per **SOP-COM-015** (Regulatory Breach Reporting) within the timeframe corresponding to the breach severity tier.

If the breach is classified Critical (Level 1) under SOP-COM-015, the two-hour internal reporting window applies in parallel with the 72-hour regulatory window — they are not substitutes.

## 7. Audit Rights

AVL retains the right to audit any Data Processor's technical and organizational measures (TOMs) on 30 days' notice. Strategic suppliers (per AVL-PUR-012) are subject to annual mandatory audit. Preferred suppliers are subject to audit at AVL's discretion. Approved suppliers are audited only following a reported incident.
