## 1. Purpose

This Regional Governance Matrix establishes the chain of command, regional authority assignments, and sign-off hierarchies for all operational, financial, and compliance decisions across AeroVelo Logistics Global's three primary operating divisions.

## 2. Regional Structure

AVL Global operates through three primary regional divisions, each with a designated Vice President of Operations holding unilateral authority over certain class of decisions within their geographic jurisdiction.

## 4. Critical Exception Protocol

When a logistics incident qualifies as a "Critical Exception" under SOP OP-204 Section 4.2 (financial impact exceeding $10,000 from a Tier-2 incident), the following applies:

1. The incident must be assigned to the Regional Operations Vice President listed in Table 2A for the applicable region.
2. The Regional Vice President must provide **formal written sign-off** before any corrective expenditure exceeding $10,000 may be authorized.
3. Verbal or phone approval is insufficient; a signed document must be filed in the AVL Global Incident Registry (GIR).
4. If the designated Regional Vice President is unavailable for more than 8 hours, the matter escalates to the Chief Operating Officer.

## 5. Regional Boundary Clarifications

- **EMEA Division** explicitly includes the Frankfurt, Amsterdam, and Dubai hubs. The Frankfurt hub falls under Marcus Vance's authority regardless of any prior contractual arrangements with local management.
- **Americas Division** covers all operations in the Western Hemisphere, including the São Paulo hub (GRU), which reports to Sarah Jenkins.
- **APAC Division** covers all operations in the Eastern Hemisphere east of the Prime Meridian, including the Tokyo (NRT), Singapore (SIN), and Sydney (SYD) hubs.
