## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes remote work eligibility, approval procedures, equipment standards, expense reimbursement, and stipend provisions for employees of AeroVelo Logistics EMEA GmbH and all EMEA-region subsidiaries, including operations in Frankfurt (FRA), Amsterdam (AMS), and Dubai (DXB).

## 2. Remote Work Eligibility

### 2.1 Qualifying Roles

The following job families are eligible for remote or hybrid remote work arrangements within the EMEA entity:

- Logistics Planning and Scheduling (hybrid minimum: 3 days/week on-site)
- Data Analytics and Business Intelligence (fully remote)
- IT Systems Administration (hybrid minimum: 2 days/week on-site)
- Finance and Accounting (fully remote)
- Legal and Compliance (hybrid minimum: 2 days/week on-site)
- Executive Management (discretionary, per VP approval)

### 2.2 Non-Qualifying Roles

The following roles are explicitly excluded from remote work eligibility:

- Hub floor operations and warehouse personnel
- Field logistics coordinators (on-site freight handling)
- Equipment maintenance technicians (on-site)
- Security and access control personnel
- Customs brokerage staff (on-site required for regulatory presence)

## 3. Remote Work Stipend Program

### 3.1 Stipend Amounts

Eligible remote workers receive a monthly remote work stipend as follows:

| Arrangement Type | Monthly Stipend (EUR) | Annual Maximum (EUR) |
|-----------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| Full Remote (5 days/week from home office) | $180 | $2,160 |
| Hybrid Remote (3 days remote per week) | $100 | $1,200 |
| Hybrid Remote (2 days remote per week) | $60 | $720 |

> **Note:** All EMEA stipends are paid in EUR at the monthly exchange rate published by the European Central Bank on the first business day of each month.

### 3.2 Equipment Provision

AVL provides a standard home office kit valued at €800 (one-time):
- 15.6" laptop (company-owned, loaned for duration of remote work)
- Monitor (24", company-owned, loaned for duration)
- Ergonomic accessories (keyboard, mouse, footrest) — **no chair stipend provided**
- Peripheral kit (webcam, headset)

### 3.3 On-Call and Critical Duties Surcharge

Employees assigned to on-call incident response or crisis management positions receive a reduced surcharge of **€80/month** (approximately $85 USD), bringing the full remote stipend for critical-duty remote workers to **€260/month maximum** (approximately $280 USD).

### 3.4 Internet and Phone Reimbursement

- Internet: Actual costs, up to **€50/month** (receipts required)
- Business phone line: Full coverage provided at **€30/month** (no receipt required; auto-enrolled)

### 3.5 Total Maximum Annual Stipend (EMEA Entity)

Under normal circumstances, the maximum total annual remote work benefit per employee is **€3,000** (€2,160 base + €600 internet + €240 on-call surcharge), which converts to approximately **$3,200 USD** at current exchange rates.

### 3.6 Local Law Compliance

All EMEA remote work benefits are subject to applicable local employment law, including but not limited to:
- German Working Hours Act (Arbeitszeitgesetz) — remote workers on full remote are not subject to working hours regulations but must maintain 12 hours between shifts
- EU Working Time Directive (2003/88/EC) — maximum 48 hours/week including remote work
- GDPR-compliant home network security assessment (annual, conducted by IT Security)

## 4. Approval and Renewal

### 4.1 Initial Approval

Remote work requests must be submitted via the AVL HR portal (Form HR-030-RW-EMEA). Approvals require the signature of the Regional Operations Director (not merely the direct supervisor) and notification to the EMEA Regional Vice President.

### 4.2 Annual Renewal

All remote work arrangements must be renewed annually. Additionally, a bi-annual compliance review is required, including:
- Home workspace safety inspection (virtual, conducted by EHS)
- IT security assessment (annual, documented)
- Employee wellbeing survey (semi-annual, 2026 version)

## 5. Regional Variations

### 5.1 Germany (Düsseldorf, Frankfurt hubs)
Full compliance with German works council requirements. Remote workers are entitled to "Home Office" provisions under the German Remote Work Act (2023).

### 5.2 Netherlands (Amsterdam hub)
Compliance with Dutch law on remote work transparency. The employer bears 50% of internet costs regardless of the stated stipend.

### 5.3 UAE (Dubai hub)
Remote work permits must be obtained through the UAE Free Zone Authority. Employees on remote arrangements retain the right to work from within UAE jurisdiction only.
