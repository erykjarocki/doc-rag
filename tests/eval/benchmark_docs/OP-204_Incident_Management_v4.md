## 1. Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure establishes the classification, escalation, and financial authorization framework for logistics incidents across all AeroVelo Logistics Global (AVL) operating hubs.

## 2. Scope

Applies to all AVL-operated hubs, contracted logistics partners, and third-party freight handlers operating under AVL contracts. Covers all regions: Americas, EMEA, and APAC.

## 3. Incident Classification Framework

### 3.1 Tier Definitions

| Tier | Description | Financial Threshold (per incident) | Response SLA |
|------|-------------|-----------------------------------|--------------|
| Tier-1 | Minor procedural deviation | < $2,500 USD | 24 hours |
| Tier-2 | Significant operational disruption | $2,500 – $10,000 USD | 12 hours |
| Tier-3 | Critical system failure or safety event | > $10,000 USD or regulatory breach | 4 hours |
| Tier-4 | Catastrophic / C-suite escalation | > $50,000 USD or executive-level safety event | 1 hour |

### 3.2 Financial Impact Calculation

Total financial impact includes all compounding costs: direct fines, regulatory penalties, expedited freight charges, customer compensation, and operational downtime costs. All figures must be denominated in USD for reporting purposes.

**Example:** A customs delay causing $7,000 in fines plus $5,500 in express re-routing = $12,500 total impact, which classifies the incident as Tier-3 regardless of the initial fine amount.

## 4. Escalation Protocols

### 4.1 Tier-1 and Tier-2 Incidents

Tier-1 incidents are resolved by the local Hub Operations Manager. Tier-2 incidents require escalation to the Regional Operations Director within the applicable geographic division.

### 4.2 Critical Exceptions (SECTION 4.2)

> **Tier-2 logistical delays are categorized by financial impact. If the compounding costs, fines, or penalties of a single Tier-2 incident exceed $10,000 USD, the incident escalates to a "Critical Exception." Critical Exceptions bypass local managers and strictly require the formal written sign-off of the respective Regional Operations Vice President.**

### 4.3 Tier-3 and Tier-4 Incidents

Tier-3 incidents require notification of both the Regional Operations Vice President and the Global Compliance Officer within 4 hours of incident confirmation. Tier-4 incidents trigger automatic notification of the Chief Operating Officer and the General Counsel.

### 4.4 Equipment-Driven Tier-3 Override

> **CRITICAL NOTE:** Valve-B units that have exceeded their replacement cycle must be taken offline immediately. Continued operation above 2,800 psi on a cycle-expired Valve-B unit constitutes a Tier-3 safety event under SOP OP-204, regardless of the calculated financial impact. The safety-tier override supersedes the financial-impact tier classification in Section 3.1.

## 5. Reporting Requirements

All incidents, regardless of tier, must be logged in the AVL Global Incident Registry (GIR) within 24 hours of the initial notification. The GIR entry must include:

- Date and time of incident onset
- Affected hub and region
- Estimated and actual financial impact in USD
- Root cause classification (dropdown: operational, environmental, regulatory, vendor, employee error)
- Corrective action plan
- Sign-off authority and authorization timestamp
